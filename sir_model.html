SIR model of covid-19 for Colombia

Implementation from scratch of a SIR model with Exponential Smoothing to forecast future infections in the population of Colombia.

The first hypothesis

The relationship between Venezuela and the rest of the world's countries was studied. To find this it was necessary to scale the data of all the countries, after that, explore the data graphically. It was observed that the graphs for each country seem to have some similarity between them, therefore, the linear correlation of each country with Venezuela was tested. It found that there is a linear relationship with certain countries, these were used as independent variables in a linear regression model. The results obtained in this way were good, but with a deficiency in the precision compared to real data.


The second thougth

Due to this, it was decided to study the case with a VARMA multivariable time series model. It was evaluated the AdFuller test for seasonality of the data and the Granger causality test for the causality relationship between the variables, The results from the tests determined that this model was not viable.

Last resort

Finally, a multivariate ARIMA model implemented that presented good results for individual predictions, but was very imprecise when combining all predictors linearly. This effect was due to the gap in the peaks of infections between each country and their variations in scale (the data being stationary). In conclusion, the data obtained show that a global model for a single country is not valid in terms of precision, but it is useful for providing indications of contagion trends. This is perfect for confirming results from local models in each country, like Holt's Exponential Smoothing, SIR or SEIR model, ARIMA and ARIMAX.


One of the biggest obstacles in this study was the irregularity of the data from Venezuela. As can be seen, they tend to be very erratic, almost random. This pattern makes it difficult to ability of the model to make accurate predictions. You can test here interactive demo dashboard of the project to go deeper on the graphs.
