DNN to predict Covid-19 in Colombia

Construction of a Deep Neural Network based on a SIR model to predict the behavior of Covid-19 in Colombia.

The Project

The implementation of neural networks is widely used to solve complex problems, such as the construction of epidemiological models. One of the best known models is the SIR, which divides the population into 3 groups, called Susceptible, Infected and Recovered. Related to each other by a set of differential equations, these are used for its construction.

However, a differential equation can be modeled, without prior knowledge, using neural networks, as explained in this article Physics-informed neural networks: A deeplearning framework for solving forward and inverse problems involving nonlinear partial differential equations. Therefore, for the Project onstruction and study of Deep Neural Networks for a SIR model of Covid-19 in Colombia, this concept was used in its development.

Development

To achieve this goal, 3 neural networks were built, each one to model each population group (Suceptible, Infected, Recovered), and each one was assigned a corresponding data set. This data was restructured to predict 10 days into the future, using 15 days into the past, these client-defined constants.

The neural networks were built as a dense network with 3 layers of 256 neurons each, with a Long short-term memory (LSTM) activation function, a Dropout layer (to avoid ovefitting) and an output neuron for the value of prediction.

Results

After training the networks, their performance was evaluated. The results can be seen in the graphs below.



We can observe that the networks show a behavior similar to that of the data, detailing how the population of recovered people has a smoother movement compared to the original data. All networks obtained an R2 score higher than 0.9, proving to be efficient and consistent with the data.

As a final thought, there is the possibility of improving the performance of the network by adjusting the days in the past that are used to make the prediction, along with the days in the future to predict. An ideal combination of these values has the potential to more accurately display future events.
