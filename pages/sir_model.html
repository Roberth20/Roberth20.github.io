SIR model of covid-19 for Colombia

Implementation from scratch of a SIR model with Exponential Smoothing to forecast future infections in the population of Colombia.

Some concepts

The objective of this project was to build and study the feasibility of the SIR epidemiological model. This one, optimized with the exponential smoothing technique to forecast future Covid-19 infections. The SIR model divides the population into subpopulations: susceptible, infected, and recovered. The main premise of this model is that people will go through each group following the steps S -> I -> R. The recovered ones are immune to re-infection, and it does not consider the natality and mortality. For more information about SIR model you can check the wikipedia website: https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modelo_SIR

On the other hand, the exponential smoothing methods are weighted averages of past observations, with the weights decaying exponentially as the observation get older. References to Hyndman, Rob J., and George Athanasopoulos. Forecasting: principles and practice. OTexts, 2014.

Development

The development of the model began to adjust SIR model into Colombia's data and on this way, it would find infection (\(\beta\)) and recovery rates (\(\gamma\)). Those are necessary to forecast. The equations that follow the model are: $$ \begin{split} \frac{dS}{dt} &= -\beta S I\\ \frac{dI}{dt} &= \beta SI - \gamma I\\ \frac{dR}{dt} &= \gamma I \end{split} $$ Subsequently, Exponential Smoothing technique was applied on infection and recovery rates to forecast future values of specified timeframe. In this examples, 20 days into the future from train set.


Forecasting cases

With the previous information, SIR model was used again to calculate each population numbers on the future. The results show a high precision for the Susceptible and Recovered populations with the selected timeframe. However, the results in the infected population show that they are viable for timeframes lesser, but forecasting 20 days into the future give us a good approximation.


The model can be tested in that interactive demo dashboard . It can be explored the data with differents train/test splits forecasting 20 days into the future.
